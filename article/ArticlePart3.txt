But lest you get too excited, it's not all fun and games and flashing lights and little sirens! Sometimes, the legislative branch takes itself seriously. In 1976, the National Emergencies Act was passed to further formalize the way Executive Orders were handled. The act ended all previously established national emergencies and also required that the president tell Congress before declaring a national emergency, permitting Congress to terminate the emergency with a joint resolution. So, get in the ambulance, but maybe stay in your lane.
